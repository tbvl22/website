On Which Data Distribution (Synthetic or Real) We Should Rely for Soft Biometric Classification

Authors: Manju RA, Atul Kumar, Akshay Agarwal

Abstract: Gender identification is vital for human-computer interaction and identity search. While "real" facial images are standard in gender classification, synthetic images are gaining attention due to privacy concerns and advancements in generative networks. However, their effectiveness remains unclear. This study evaluates the performance of gender classification networks trained on real vs. synthetic face images. Using 8 Deep Neural Networks (DNNs), including CNNs and ViTs, across 4 datasets and 6 image corruptions, we also employ Grad-CAM and t-SNE for interpretability. The research assesses synthetic data's reliability in reflecting real-world distributions and resilience to perturbations.

Corruption Depth: Analysis of DNN Depth

Authors: Akshay Agarwal, Nalini Ratha

Abstract: We introduce the concept of corruption depth to identify the network layers where misclassification due to input corruptions occurs. This novel approach provides insights into how corruptions affect specific layers, enabling targeted pruning instead of computationally expensive full-network purification. Our study explores the relationship between input complexity, noise, and network depth, presenting a framework to analyze classification progress through the network. This understanding enhances model explainability and compression, offering a new perspective on query difficulty and example memorization.

Indian Traffic Sign Detection and Classification Through a Unified Framework

Authors: Rishabh Uikey, Haroon R. Lone, Akshay Agarwal

Abstract: Traffic signboards play a crucial role in smart transportation systems, with inattentiveness to these boards causing over 90% of accidents. Addressing the unique challenges of Indian traffic signboards, we propose a novel deep neural network architecture for simultaneous detection and classification. Our method leverages state-of-the-art backbones like AlexNet, VGG-19, ResNet-50, and EfficientNet v2 to achieve detection and classification accuracies of 85.5% and 98.5% on a newly collected Indian traffic sign dataset with 4257 images. Comprehensive evaluations on both the collected and existing datasets highlight the effectiveness of our approach compared to state-of-the-art methods.

A Generalized Semiconductor Wafer Defect Classifier

Authors: P. Rai, P. Pal, A. Agarwal

Abstract: Silicon-based integrated circuits (ICs) and electronic devices are used in every possible electronic device, including high-performance computers fabricated from silicon wafers. Hence, ensuring the quality and reliability of silicon components is of utmost importance. This work focuses on developing and implementing computer vision and deep learning algorithms to detect defects in semiconductor manufacturing ICs, contributing to higher yields and reduced production costs.
