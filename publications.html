Publications

On Which Data Distribution (Synthetic or Real) We Should Rely for Soft Biometric Classification

Authors: Manju RA, Atul Kumar, Akshay Agarwal

Abstract: Gender identification is vital for human-computer interaction and identity search. While "real" facial images are standard in gender classification, synthetic images are gaining attention due to privacy concerns and advancements in generative networks. However, their effectiveness remains unclear. This study evaluates the performance of gender classification networks trained on real vs. synthetic face images. Using 8 DNNs, including CNNs and ViTs, across 4 datasets and 6 image corruptions, we also employ Grad-CAM and t-SNE for interpretability.

Corruption Depth: Analysis of DNN Depth

Authors: Akshay Agarwal, Nalini Ratha

Abstract: We introduce the concept of corruption depth to identify network layers where misclassification due to input corruptions occurs. This approach provides insights into corruption effects and enables targeted pruning over full-network purification.

Indian Traffic Sign Detection and Classification Through a Unified Framework

Authors: Rishabh Uikey, Haroon R. Lone, Akshay Agarwal

Abstract: Traffic signboards are crucial for smart transportation. We propose a novel DNN architecture for simultaneous detection and classification, leveraging state-of-the-art backbones for high accuracies.

A Generalized Semiconductor Wafer Defect Classifier

Authors: P. Rai, P. Pal, A. Agarwal

Abstract: This work develops computer vision and deep learning algorithms to detect defects in semiconductor manufacturing, improving yields and reducing production costs.
