Vishesh Kumar, and Akshay Agarwal

International Conference on Machine Learning (ICML) 2026

Abstract: Despite decades of progress in neural network training, robustness to natural corruptions remains an unresolved challenge across architectures, from CNNs and transformers to large foundation models. We show that such corruptions often drive internal representations into a high-entropy state, making predictions depend on a small set of fragile features. To address this, we propose Dem-HEC, an entropy-guided fine-tuning framework that generates bounded high-entropy samples and uses consistency learning with teacher distillation to improve corruption robustness while preserving, and often improving, clean accuracy across diverse datasets and models, including DINOv3.

The Unseen Adversaries: Robust and Generalized Defense Against Adversarial Patches (2026)

Vishesh Kumar, and Akshay Agarwal

International Conference on Artificial Intelligence and Statistics (AISTATS) 2026

Abstract: Deep neural networks remain highly vulnerable to real-world singularities such as adversarial patch attacks and natural noise corruptions (e.g., Gaussian and Salt-and-Pepper noise), yet prior work largely studies them in isolation. We present the first benchmark that combines both singularities through a novel dataset and evaluate singularity detection using CNN-based features with traditional machine learning classifiers. Extensive in-distribution and OOD experiments reveal that defending against these perturbations independently, and with poorly chosen classifiers, leads to weak robustness.

Tap, Scan, Exploit: The Hidden Vulnerabilities of Everyday QR Codes

Ashish Kumar, S Aarthi, and Akshay Agarwal

IEEE/CVF Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition (CVPR 2026) Findings

Abstract: QR codes are widely used for payments, marketing, authentication, and information sharing. However, their decodability is prone to real-world distortions, leading to issues when users cannot scan QR codes in critical situations. Thus, we bring a rarely explored approach to investigate QR robustness in several real-life adverse situations. We examine the robustness of QR code decoding under 18 distinct image corruption types. We have not only studied the impact of these distortions but also the effect of local regions of a QR code by modifying QRs at the global and regional levels. To pave the way for future secure digital communication involving QR codes, we have conducted a preliminary study demonstrating the impact of generative networks on mitigating corruption.

Guarding Digital Identity: Attention-Guided Fusion for Detecting Forged ID Documents (Student Abstract)

Gargi Surendra Yeole, Poulomi Bhattacharya, and Akshay Agarwal

Proceedings of the AAAI Conference on Artificial Intelligence

Abstract: This paper proposes an ID forgery detection framework for government e-verification pipelines that analyzes mobile-captured ID images and detects highly realistic, generative forgeries across both facial and textual regions. The method integrates feature fusion and attention mechanisms over CNNs (ResNet‑50, EfficientNet) and transformers (ViT‑16, Swin Transformer) to learn robust visual representations for reliable real‑world deployment.

WingBeats and Snapshots: Fusing Sound and Vision for Mosquito Monitoring (Student Abstract)

Ahana Chanda, and Akshay Agarwal

Proceedings of the AAAI Conference on Artificial Intelligence

Abstract: Accurate mosquito species identification is vital for vector control, yet visual or acoustic cues alone are often inadequate. This work introduces a multimodal deep-learning framework that fuses high-resolution images with wingbeat audio via a SwinV2 vision transformer and an Audio Spectrogram Transformer, exploiting complementary information. On a six-species dataset, it reaches 97% accuracy, matching the best single-modality baseline while offering greater robustness to noise and environmental variation for reliable mosquito surveillance.

Semantic-Guided Sketch-to-RGB Image Generation via Controlled Diffusion for Improved Sketch Recognition (Student Abstract)

Ritika Jain, Atul Kumar, and Akshay Agarwal

Proceedings of the AAAI Conference on Artificial Intelligence

Abstract: This work introduces a sketch-to-RGB pipeline that uses BLIP-based semantic captions and Canny edges from a sketch to guide a Stable Diffusion model via ControlNet, producing photorealistic, identity-preserving images. By training standard CNN and transformer classifiers on Tiny-ImageNet subsets and testing on sketches, synthesized images, and original RGB data, the method significantly boosts sketch classification accuracy and generalizes well across architectures.

Traces Left Behind: Deepfake Detection Under the Lens of Iterative Generation

Poulomi Bhattacharya, Akshay Agarwal, and Nalini Ratha

Proceedings of the Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition Workshop (AIMS)

Abstract: This work investigates a more realistic and powerful threat model in which attackers sequentially apply multiple heterogeneous generators to create iteratively manipulated deepfakes, rather than relying on a single model. It analyzes how the composition and ordering of these generative steps alter visual and representational characteristics, and shows through extensive experiments that such iterative deepfakes can more easily evade state-of-the-art detectors trained only on single-step manipulations, revealing a critical vulnerability in current deepfake detection systems.

Faces in the Wild: GAN-Driven Normalization for Robust Facial Recognition

Mohit, Atul Kumar, and Akshay Agarwal

Proceedings of the Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition Workshop (CVEU)

Abstract: Facial recognition systems often struggle with pose and expression variations in real-world settings. We propose a two-stage preprocessing pipeline using Pixel2Style2Pixel for pose normalization and StarGAN for emotion neutralization before recognition. We evaluate our approach across 10 facial recognition models using DeepFace on the Karolinska Directed Emotional Faces (KDEF) dataset. The results show significant robustness improvements for lightweight models while also revealing identity-preservation challenges in high-capacity models, highlighting the need for adaptive preprocessing strategies.

Q-MoFusion: A Quantum Classifier for Mosquito Species Classification (Student Abstract)

Vishesh Kumar, Ahana Chanda, Poulomi Bhattacharya, Akshay Agarwal

Proceedings of the AAAI Conference on Artificial Intelligence (AAAI),2026

Abstract: Q-MoFusion is a hybrid quantum-classical mosquito identification framework that fuses AST and Whisper features using a Variational Quantum Circuit, achieving superior accuracy and robustness, especially on imbalanced bioacoustic data.

Improving CAPTCHA Robustness via Controlled Image Corruptions (Student Abstract)

Suchetan G. Uppur, Ashish Kumar, Akshay Agarwal

Proceedings of the AAAI Conference on Artificial Intelligence (AAAI),2026

Abstract: This work evaluates how image corruptions (blur, noise, elastic transforms, occlusion) affect human and AI CAPTCHA solving, benchmarking MLLMs, OCR models, and humans to reveal vulnerabilities and improve CAPTCHA robustness.

Unmasking the Audio Illusion: A Survey on Spoofing and Deepfake Detection

Aarthi S, and Akshay Agarwal

IEEE International Joint Conference on Biometrics

Abstract: This paper explores the growing practice of manipulating audio to create fakes using deep learning algorithms. It examines methods like speech synthesis, audio splicing, and voice cloning, and various detection algorithms. The paper also discusses the potential impacts of deepfake audio on reputation, fraud, and misinformation, and compares AI and human detection methods.

Robustness Benchmarking of Convolutional and Transformer Architectures for Image Classification

Vishesh Kumar, Shivam Shukla, and Akshay Agarwal

IEEE Transactions on Big Data

Abstract: A study analyzing deep convolutional neural networks (CNNs) across various classification spectrums, including pure CNNs and state-of-the-art Vision Transformers (ViTs), found that different DNNs have varying levels of robustness against image corruption. ViTs, trained on CIFAR10, are highly robust in handling noise corruptions but vulnerable to environmental corruptions. Pure CNNs, while lower in performance, can handle environmental factors better than noise corruption. The study aims to reveal the vulnerabilities of deep learning models and ensure the deployment of correct networks for real-world image classification.

Your Face, Your Privacy: Combating Unauthorized Usage

Atul Kumar, Akshay Agarwal, and Nalini Ratha

IEEE International Conference on Automatic Face and Gesture Recognition (FG)

Abstract: Deep face recognition systems' high performance raises privacy concerns. Users are exploring ways to protect themselves, such as obscuring faces with hands. However, no evaluation exists to show this can bypass face recognition algorithms. A study developed nose and mouth occlusion datasets using synthetic patches and real-life objects. Results showed that patches can fool face recognition networks and soft biometric classifiers, deceiving gender and ethnicity detection.

Gesture Recognition for Emergencies: Dataset and Cross-Condition Analysis

Jiya Sinha, Poulomi Bhattacharya, Akshay Agarwal

IEEE International Conference on Automatic Face and Gesture Recognition (FG)

Abstract: Gestures are crucial for nonverbal communication, especially in areas like home automation and medical applications. Research in computer vision, robotics, and HCI has focused on gesture recognition. However, existing studies have limited exploration of Indian ethnicity and gesture capture in nighttime settings. A large-scale hand gesture recognition dataset has been curated, and a benchmark study using state-of-the-art hand detection and gesture classification networks was conducted.

Family Resemblance or Fraud? Face Morphing Attacks on Kinship Verification

Gargi S Yeole, Aarthi S, Shalvika Srivastav, Akshay Agarwal

Second Workshop on Interdisciplinary Applications of Biometrics and Identity Science in IEEE International Conference on Automatic Face and Gesture Recognition

Abstract: This study explores the vulnerability of deep learning models for kinship verification using facial images, particularly when morphing ratios are used. The researchers used a morphed dataset to test their models on original and morphed child images. The results showed a continuous increase in misclassification rates with the increasing percentage of parental features, highlighting the need for more effective verification methods to counter facial morphing risks in real-world applications.

Advancing Facial Age Progression for Occluded Faces

Ankit Birla, Akshay Agarwal

CVPR Workshop and Competition on Affective & Behavior Analysis in-the-wild

Abstract: Face recognition is vulnerable when the age gap between gallery and probe images is high. Accurate age progression is crucial to mitigate this gap and improve face recognition performance. This paper presents a novel approach to facial age progression, addressing occluded faces. Objects occluding the face's key points are detected and inpainted using transformer architecture. This improves facial age progression's robustness and visual quality, making it applicable in security systems and forensic analysis.

Bayesian‑optimized recursive machine learning for predicting human‑induced changes in suspended sediment transport

Soumya Kundu, Somil Swarnkar, Akshay Agarwal

Environmental Monitoring and Assessment

Abstract: This study examines historical changes in suspended sediment load (SSL) in the Godavari River Basin, revealing a decline post-1990 due to human activities. Tree-based machine learning models improved SSL prediction accuracy, providing insights for sustainable sediment transport modeling under changing hydrological conditions.

A Unified, Resilient, and Explainable Adversarial Patch Detector

Vishesh Kumar, Akshay Agarwal

IEEE/CVF Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition (CVPR)

Abstract: The AdvPatchXAI is a defense algorithm designed to protect Deep Neural Networks (DNNs) from physical adversarial attacks. It uses a novel patch decorrelation loss, self-supervised learning, and a sparse linear layer for classification. The model improves interpretability and correlation with human vision, closing the semantic gap between latent and pixel space and effectively handling unseen patches, thereby enhancing DNN robustness in practical settings.

Detection of identity swapping attacks in low-resolution image settings

Akshay Agarwal, Nalini Ratha

Journal of Information Security and Applications

Abstract: This research proposes a low-resolution identity swap attack detection algorithm to address the challenge of detecting fake face images in low-resolution videos. The algorithm uses artifacts amplification and classification to handle the lack of information content. Extensive evaluations using multiple databases, resolution settings, and attack types demonstrate the strength and effectiveness of the proposed algorithm in in-the-wild settings. The results show superiority compared to existing state-of-the-art works.

On Which Data Distribution (Synthetic or Real) We Should Rely for Soft Biometric Classification

Manju RA, Atul Kumar, Akshay Agarwal

Winter Conference on Applications of Computer Vision (WACV)

Abstract: Gender identification is vital for human-computer interaction and identity search. While "real" facial images are standard in gender classification, synthetic images are gaining attention due to privacy concerns and advancements in generative networks. However, their effectiveness remains unclear. This study evaluates the performance of gender classification networks trained on real vs. synthetic face images. Using 8 DNNs, including CNNs and ViTs, across 4 datasets and 6 image corruptions, we also employ Grad-CAM and t-SNE for interpretability.

PrecipFormer: Efficient Transformer for Precipitation Downscaling

R. Kumar, T. Sharma, V. Vaghela, S. Jha, A. Agarwal

Winter Conference on Applications of Computer Vision (WACV) Workshops

Abstract: Precipitation downscaling is a crucial challenge in climate modeling and hydrological applications. This paper introduces PrecipFormer, a computationally efficient transformer architecture designed for this task. It builds upon the Low-to-High Multi-Level Vision Transformer mechanism, enabling parallel processing of features at multiple spatial scales and reducing computational overhead. PrecipFormer achieves superior performance compared to state-of-the-art baselines.

Identity in the Blood Relation: Unraveling the Complexity of Morph Detection in Kinship Biometrics

Shalvika Srivastav, Poulomi Bhattacharya, Akshay Agarwal, Nalini Ratha

PFATCV Workshop in British Machine Vision Conference (BMVC) 2025

Abstract:This work studies kinship-based face morphing attacks by creating a large synthetic dataset (OpenCV + latent autoencoder morphs) and proposing a FaceNet–ResNet50 hybrid detection framework to evaluate detection and recognition performance across varying morph ratios and unseen conditions.

Brain Matters: Enhancing Tumor Classification via CNN and Vision-Language Fusion

Chaudhari Khushi Ganesh, Akshay Agarwal

BMVC workshop 2025

Abstract: This work proposes a hybrid brain tumor MRI classification framework that fuses Vision-Language Model embeddings (BiomedCLIP/UniMedCLIP) with ResNet spatial features using weighted concatenation to improve accuracy and overcome zero-shot limitations across multiple datasets.

On Visual Saliency Maps for Identifying Fidelity of Deepfake Detection Datasets

Agniva Banerjee, Samiran Das, Akshay Agarwal

BMVC Workshop 2025

Abstract: This paper uses explainable AI methods (SHAP, LIME, IG) to localize manipulated regions in deepfake images and shows through cross-dataset experiments that deepfake-trained models generalize better than Photoshop-forgery-trained models.

On Adversarial Robustness of Face Presentation Attack Detection Algorithms

Akshay Agarwal, Mayank Vatsa, Richa Singh

Proceedings of the IEEE/CVF International Conference on Computer Vision (ICCV) 2025

Abstract: This work shows that combined presentation + adversarial multi-point attacks can jointly fool face recognition systems and proposes universal defense strategies that outperform existing methods against both attack types.

On the Robustness of Iris Presentation Attack Detectors

Aditya Sneh, Akshay Agarwal

Available at SSRN 4918189, 2025

Abstract: This work is the first to systematically evaluate iris presentation attack detection (IPAD) robustness under novel image corruptions, showing that even blind pixel perturbations can drastically degrade SOTA performance (e.g., ViT EER rises from 7.78% to 58.36%).
